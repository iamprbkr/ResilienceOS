# Product Strategy

## MVP Definition

The MVP should prove that customers can plan, run, measure, and report resilience simulations across cyber, AI governance, crisis, and compliance.

Included:

- Scenario library and builder
- Live exercise timeline
- Inject management console
- Role-based dashboards
- AI-assisted inject generation
- Standards mapping
- Resilience scorecards
- After-action report generation
- Tenant/RBAC foundations

Deferred:

- Full customer SSO matrix
- Native Neo4j graph editor
- Custom model fine-tuning
- Multi-region active-active deployment
- Deep third-party GRC integrations

## Roadmap

Phase 1: MVP command center, scenario library, scoring, report exports.

Phase 2: AI facilitator, adaptive branching, websocket live sessions, SSO, audit-grade logs.

Phase 3: Neo4j standards graph, vector retrieval, customer playbook ingestion, evidence packs.

Phase 4: Sovereign deployments, private model gateways, sector packs for healthcare, BFSI, telecom, government.

Phase 5: Marketplace of simulation packs, partner integrations, benchmarking network.

## Pricing Strategy

- Launch: $36k/year, 3 exercise teams, core modules, standard reports.
- Scale: $120k/year, unlimited scenarios, AI facilitator, SSO, integrations, advanced analytics.
- Sovereign: $300k+/year, private deployment, customer-managed keys, region pinning, premium support.
- Services: paid onboarding, custom sector packs, board simulations, and annual resilience maturity assessment.

## Competitive Positioning

The product is not a cyber range and not a generic GRC tool. It sits between incident response exercising, AI governance assurance, operational resilience analytics, and executive crisis simulation.

Differentiators:

- Human-AI trust boundary simulations
- AI-native threat and governance failures
- Executive and SOC convergence
- Cross-domain standards mapping
- Adaptive facilitator agents
- Operational resilience scoring instead of checklist-only compliance

## Investor Pitch Narrative

Enterprise resilience has become a board-level AI, cyber, regulatory, and operational problem. Existing tools separate cyber ranges, GRC platforms, continuity planning, and executive tabletop exercises. This platform unifies those workflows into an AI-native simulation ecosystem that trains organizations before real incidents force the lesson. The wedge is high-value AI governance and cyber resilience exercising; the expansion path is resilience intelligence across regulated enterprises and government-grade deployments.
